The Benefits of Sending Your Child to Preschool

The brain develops at its most rapid pace in the first few years of life. Research shows that children are born learning and that their early years of life significantly impact their elementary, middle, high school, and college experiences. The first five years are when children develop the essential emotional and social skills they need to succeed in a school environment. Our early childhood school aims to provide your child with the environment, activities and lessons they need, early on.

Stimulates Brain Development

Activities in preschool stimulate young children’s brains and contribute to their intellectual, and social development. Between the ages of 3 and 5, the vocabulary of a child grows significantly, and their sentences become longer and more complex. They have more opportunities to sing, read, and converse with students and teachers while being introduced to new vocabulary.

Helps Your Child Socially & Emotionally

In preschool, children learn cooperation, respect, and problem-solving skills while interacting with other children and adults. Preschool provides a safe place for your child to learn that they are capable of doing things on their own, and a place where they can learn how to build relationships with other people.

Prepares Your Child for Kindergarten

As kindergarten becomes more academic, preschool can be the path to success in the future. Children who attend preschool are generally more eager to learn and are able to handle social interactions and group dynamics better.

Provides a Structured Environment

Preschool allows children to be in a structured setting that teaches them to learn, share things, take turns, follow instructions and share their teacher’s attention with peers. It is beneficial for a child to have group experience like this prior to starting kindergarten.
